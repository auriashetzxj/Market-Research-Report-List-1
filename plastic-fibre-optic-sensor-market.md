<p><p>The plastic fiber optic sensor market is primarily categorized into two types: Thru-beam and Reflective sensors. Thru-beam sensors consist of a transmitter and receiver positioned opposite each other, allowing continuous light transmission and detection of interruptions, ideal for object detection over long distances. In contrast, Reflective sensors utilize a single unit that emits light and detects reflections off nearby objects, making them suitable for proximity sensing applications. Both types leverage the benefits of plastic fiber optics, including flexibility and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>

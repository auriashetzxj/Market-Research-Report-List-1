<p><p>In-Mould Labels (IML) are labels that are applied to containers and products during the manufacturing process, specifically during the injection or blow moulding of plastic items. This process involves placing a pre-printed label into a mould before the plastic is introduced, allowing the label to become an integrated part of the product. IML offers advantages such as durability, enhanced aesthetic appeal, and resistance to scratching and fading, making it ideal for various applications, including food packaging, cosmetics, and household goods.</p><p>The In-Mould Labels (IML) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.6% during the forecast period. <p><p>In-Mould Labels (IML) are created by placing labels within a mold during the manufacturing process, providing a durable and visually appealing finish. The market can be categorized into three main types: Injection Molding, which uses high-temperature processes to form plastic, Extrusion-Blow Molding, where plastic is extruded and inflated into a mold for hollow shapes, and Thermoforming, where plastic sheets are heated and shaped over molds. Each type offers unique benefits for packaging and product design.</p></p>

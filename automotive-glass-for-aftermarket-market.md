<p><p>In the aftermarket automotive glass market, three primary types are prevalent: laminated glass, tempered glass, and others. Laminated glass consists of two or more layers bonded with a polymer interlayer, providing safety and sound insulation. Tempered glass is heat-treated for increased strength and shatters into small, less harmful pieces upon impact. The "others" segment includes specialized glass types like coated or acoustic glass, catering to specific vehicle needs and enhancing performance features. Each type addresses different safety and aesthetic requirements in vehicle repair and replacement.</p></p>

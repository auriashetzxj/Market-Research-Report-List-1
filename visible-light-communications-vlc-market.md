<p><p>Visible Light Communications (VLC) is a technology that uses light, typically from LED sources, to transmit data wirelessly. The VLC market can be divided into two main types: Access Points and Station Points. Access Points act as transmission hubs that send data through light beams to multiple users, while Station Points refer to receiving units that decode the transmitted information. This dual structure facilitates effective communication in various environments, enhancing connectivity in areas where traditional wireless technologies may face limitations.</p></p>

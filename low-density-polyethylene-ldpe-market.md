<p><p>Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE) is a versatile plastic known for its flexibility and resilience. The LDPE market is primarily segmented into two production methods: the autoclave process and the tubular process. The autoclave process involves polymerization in a high-pressure reactor, resulting in controlled molecular weight and properties. In contrast, the tubular process utilizes a continuous flow reactor, offering higher production efficiency and reduced cycle times. Both methods cater to diverse applications, including packaging, containers, and film production, driven by varying market demands.</p></p>
